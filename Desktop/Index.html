MY FAVOURITE STREET FOODS

(1) MISAL PAV :

Misal pav (Marathi: मिसळपाव) is a dish from the Indian state of Maharashtra. It consists of misal (a spicy curry usually made from moth beans) and pav (a type of Indian bread roll). The final dish is topped with farsan or sev, onions, lemon and coriander (cilantro).

Ingridients :

It consists of misal (a spicy curry usually made from moth beans) and pav (a type of Indian bread roll). The final dish is topped with farsan or sev, onions, lemon and coriander (cilantro).

Nutritions :

Misal pav is rich in protein and fiber, and it's high in Vitamin B1 and folic acid.

(2) PANI PURI :

Pani puri, also known as gol gappa, is a popular Indian snack or street food that consists of a hollow, crispy-fried ball filled with a variety of ingredients and then popped into the mouth:

Ingredients :

Hollow, crispy-fried ball filled with potatoes, chickpeas, onions, spices, and flavored water

Nutritions :

(3) DABELI :

Dabeli originated in the Kutch region of Gujarat. The word "dabeli" means "pressed".

Ingredients :

Dabeli is made with a spicy, sweet, and tangy potato filling sandwiched between soft buns. The filling is typically cooked with a blend of spices called Dabeli Masala, which includes cumin, coriander, and red chili powder. Dabeli is also served with chutneys, peanuts, pomegranate seeds, and sev (crunchy chickpea noodles).

Nutritions :

A dabeli contains around 199 calories, with 47 calories from carbohydrates, 12 calories from protein, and 142 calories from fats
